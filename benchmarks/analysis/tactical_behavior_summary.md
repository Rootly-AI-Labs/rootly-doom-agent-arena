## Safest Behavioral Findings For The Paper

- Accepted intent calls are dominated by `engage_opponent`, with `strafe_attack` and `search` used as secondary modes.
- Requested fire control is mostly conservative: `only_when_aligned` is the largest fire-policy bucket overall, while `suppressive` and `burst_when_aligned` appear in close/evasive policies.
- Requested distance control is dominated by `close` and `maintain`; `kite` appears as a smaller but meaningful evasive policy.
- Requested LOS recovery is recorded through `los_lost_action`; `sweep` and `advance_last_seen` are the main observed choices.

## Tentative Findings

- Differences in tactical distributions may reflect both model behavior and side/state exposure, especially because the benchmark has a known player-side skew.
- Actual stuck recovery events are autopilot/environment outcomes, not pure model decisions, so they should be interpreted as interaction effects.

## Appendix-Only Metrics

- Autopilot action/reason distributions are useful diagnostics but should stay out of headline model comparisons because they are generated by the Doom-side controller.
- `replan_if` is **UNKNOWN_NOT_RECORDED_IN_STATS_CALLS** for this run; it should not be used as a paper metric unless future runs record it per intent.
